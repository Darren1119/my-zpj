## Design Context

### Users
Primary audience is recruiters, hiring managers, and design leads reviewing a UX designer's portfolio. They need to quickly understand the designer's strengths, see evidence through selected projects, and get a clear sense of process, communication style, and product thinking.

### Brand Personality
Calm, bold, and approachable. The site should feel confident and contemporary without becoming cold or overly self-serious. It should signal strong design taste, structured thinking, and collaborative energy.

### Aesthetic Direction
An editorial portfolio with a modern product-design edge. The experience should be light-mode first, spacious, and typographically expressive, with restrained but memorable color. It should avoid generic startup gradients, template-like card grids, and overly decorative portfolio tropes.

### Design Principles
1. Show clarity fast: communicate role, strengths, and featured work within the first screen.
2. Let structure build trust: make process, outcomes, and thinking easy to scan.
3. Balance restraint with surprise: keep the interface calm, but introduce bold composition and motion in a deliberate way.
4. Stay human: use copy and interactions that feel collaborative and warm, not distant or self-promotional.
5. Design for hiring behavior: help busy reviewers quickly understand fit and know what to click next.
